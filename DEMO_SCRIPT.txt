[0:00 — HOOK & PROBLEM STATEMENT]
Hello everyone. Reading and implementing academic research papers can be extremely daunting for students, junior developers, and early-career researchers. When approaching a new paper, they get stuck on complex mathematical equations and academic terminology. They struggle to identify the exact prerequisites they need to study first, have difficulty discovering accessible datasets, and cannot estimate the real-world engineering complexity of building the models.

[0:30 — RESEARCH NAVIGATOR AI OVERVIEW]
That is why we built Research Navigator AI. It acts as an intelligent research mentor that turns passive paper reading into active project-based learning. Rather than just summarizing text, it dynamically evaluates the user's readiness level, maps out a concrete weekly learning path, queries dataset availability, and suggests personalized beginner, intermediate, and advanced practical projects.

[1:00 — SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE WALKTHROUGH]
(Show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let's walk through our system architecture. The user interacts with our React frontend. The query is received by the custom FastAPI bridge, which routes the execution through the ADK workflow graph.
First, the Security Checkpoint node sanitizes inputs, scrubbing PII and checking Regex filters to immediately block prompt injections, jailbreaks, and shell/script execution attempts (like rm -rf or subprocess calls) without wasting LLM tokens. 
If it passes, the Coordinator Agent takes over. To solve the strict API rate limits and 503 overloads typical of nested agent roundtrips, our Coordinator is grounded directly via 7 local FastMCP database tools. It queries prerequisites, datasets, complexity, and roadmaps concurrently, generating a comprehensive 10-section report in a single, fast LLM turn.
The workflow then enters a Human Review Node where the execution suspends, prompting the user for approval or adjustment feedback before completing.
